# T20 vs ODI vs Test (What changes and why it matters)

This note explains the practical differences between formats and how they affect strategy and stats.

## Overs and innings structure

- **T20**: 20 overs per side (limited overs).
- **ODI**: 50 overs per side (limited overs).
- **Test**: no fixed over limit per innings; matches can last up to 5 days.

## Fielding restrictions and phases

- Limited-overs formats have fielding restrictions (powerplays). This changes:
  - boundary opportunities
  - risk/reward of attacking early
  - how teams plan matchups

See: `rules/powerplay.md`, `tactics/batting_phases.md`

## What “good” stats mean depends on format

### Batting strike rate

- A strike rate of 135 can be strong in **T20**, average in some high-scoring eras/venues, and unusually high in **ODIs**.
- In **Tests**, strike rate matters, but patience/time at the crease can be equally important.

### Bowling economy

- In **T20**, economy is heavily phase-dependent (death overs are harder).
- In **ODI**, bowlers may trade wickets vs run control in different phases.
- In **Tests**, economy exists but is often interpreted alongside match context (conditions, new ball spells, etc.).

See: `stats/strike_rate.md`, `stats/economy.md`

## Common confusion: comparing across formats

- “Best batter” or “best bowler” depends on:
  - format
  - era (rules and scoring patterns change)
  - venue/pitch
  - role (opener vs finisher; powerplay bowler vs death bowler)

For stats-based comparisons, always specify: format + timeframe + role/phase.
