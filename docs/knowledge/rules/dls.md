# DLS (Duckworth–Lewis–Stern) — simple explanation

DLS is a method used in limited-overs cricket (ODI/T20) to set fair targets when overs are reduced (usually due to rain).

## Intuition: resources

Teams have two main “resources” to score runs:
- **overs** (balls left)
- **wickets in hand**

If a match is shortened, teams may have fewer overs, which changes their scoring potential. DLS adjusts targets using a model of how scoring potential changes with overs and wickets.

## What changes in a rain-affected match

- The chasing team may get fewer overs.
- The target is recalculated so that both teams’ opportunities are comparable.

## Common confusions

- “But team A had more overs earlier!”  
  DLS tries to account for the fact that scoring potential changes through an innings and depends on wickets lost.

- “Why did the target go up when overs reduced?”  
  It can happen if the chasing team is ahead of the par score at interruption, or if wicket/over resources are re-evaluated.

## Practical takeaway

For most fans: DLS is a fairness adjustment based on *time/balls remaining and wickets left*.
