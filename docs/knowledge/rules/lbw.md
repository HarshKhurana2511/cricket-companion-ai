# LBW (Leg Before Wicket) — simple explanation

LBW is a dismissal where the ball hits the batter’s body (usually the pad) **instead of the bat**, and the umpire judges that the ball would have hit the stumps.

## Key idea

LBW exists to prevent batters from using their pads as a “second bat”.

## The common conditions (high level)

An LBW is usually given if all are true:

1) The ball hits the batter (pad/body) and **not the bat first**.
2) The ball would have gone on to hit the stumps.
3) Where the ball pitched and where it hit the pad matter:
   - e.g., if it pitched outside leg stump, LBW is generally not given.

Exact edge cases depend on playing conditions and umpire judgment.

## Common confusions

- **Inside edge**: If the ball hits the bat first, LBW is not given (even if it later hits the pad).
- **Impact outside off stump**: In many cases, a batter offering a shot changes the decision logic.

For basic understanding, focus on the core concept: *pad stops a ball that would have hit the stumps*.
