# Powerplay (field restrictions) — basics

Powerplays are periods in limited-overs cricket with restrictions on how many fielders can be outside the inner circle.

## Why powerplays exist

- To encourage attacking cricket early (more boundaries).
- To balance bat vs ball by limiting defensive fields.

## T20 powerplay (typical)

- Usually the first **6 overs**.
- Fielding restrictions mean more gaps and more boundary-scoring opportunities.

## Strategy implications

### Batting

- Higher boundary potential, but early wickets can hurt.
- Teams often send aggressive batters early.

### Bowling

- Bowlers aim for wickets and containment (swing/seam early).
- Mistakes get punished more due to field restrictions.

## Note

Rules can vary slightly by competition/season. For analytics, always specify format and era if rules are critical.
