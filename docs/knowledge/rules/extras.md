# Extras (wide, no-ball, byes, leg-byes) — basics

Extras are runs that do **not** come off the bat in the usual way. They still count to the team total.

## Wide

- A **wide** is an illegal delivery that is too wide (or otherwise not playable) as judged by the umpire.
- The batting team gets:
  - **1 run** (wide) plus any additional runs completed (or boundary, depending on the case).
- A wide **does not count as a legal ball** in the over, so the bowler must re-bowl the delivery.

## No-ball

- A **no-ball** is an illegal delivery (e.g., overstepping, dangerous delivery, etc.).
- The batting team gets:
  - **1 run** (no-ball) plus runs scored off the delivery.
- A no-ball **does not count as a legal ball** in the over, so the ball is re-bowled.
- In limited-overs cricket, a no-ball often triggers a **free hit** on the next legal delivery (rules vary by competition).

## Byes

- **Byes** are runs taken when the ball passes the batter and is not hit (and it was not a wide/no-ball).
- Byes count as extras and do not add to the batter’s runs.

## Leg-byes

- **Leg-byes** are runs taken when the ball hits the batter (body) without being hit by the bat (and it was not a wide/no-ball).
- Leg-byes count as extras and do not add to the batter’s runs.

## Why legality matters for stats

Many statistics depend on *legal balls*:
- **Overs** are counted as 6 legal balls.
- **Economy rate** (bowling) is based on runs conceded per over of legal balls.
- **Balls faced** (batting) are typically legal balls faced (wides do not count as a ball faced).
