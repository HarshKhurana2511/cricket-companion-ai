# Super over — basics

A super over is a tie-breaker used in limited-overs cricket when a match is tied (rules can vary by competition).

## What happens

- Each team plays a short “mini-innings” (often 1 over).
- The team with the higher score in the super over wins.

## Notes

- Player eligibility and the number of wickets allowed can vary by playing conditions.
- In analytics, super overs are often treated separately from “normal” innings because:
  - it’s a very small sample
  - scoring patterns are extreme
  - phase-based metrics (powerplay/middle/death) don’t apply cleanly
