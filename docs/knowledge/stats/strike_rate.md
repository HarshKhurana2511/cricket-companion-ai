# Strike rate — definition and interpretation

Strike rate is usually a batting metric:

> **Batting strike rate** = runs scored per 100 balls faced

## What “balls faced” means

In standard cricket scoring:
- wides do **not** count as balls faced (because they are illegal deliveries)
- no-balls also create an extra delivery in the over; interpretations can vary by dataset, but the common approach is to count only legal balls as balls faced

So a common formula is:

- **Strike rate** = 100 * runs_off_bat / legal_balls_faced

## Why strike rate depends on role and phase

- Openers may have higher SR in powerplay due to field restrictions.
- Middle-order batters may balance strike rotation and boundary hitting.
- Finishers may have very high SR in death overs, with higher dismissal risk.

## Common pitfalls

- Small sample sizes (e.g., 10–20 balls) can create extreme strike rates.
- Comparing SR across formats without context.
