# Economy rate — definition and interpretation

Economy rate is a bowling metric:

> **Economy** = runs conceded per over

## What “per over” means

An over is **6 legal balls**. Illegal deliveries (like wides and no-balls) do not count as legal balls and therefore do not reduce the number of balls remaining in the over.

So a typical definition is:

- **Overs bowled** = legal_balls / 6
- **Economy** = runs_conceded / overs_bowled

## Why economy must be phase-aware in T20

Economy varies strongly by phase:
- powerplay overs can have more boundaries due to field restrictions
- middle overs can be slower due to spread fields/spin
- death overs are typically the highest scoring

So “economy” should be interpreted along with:
- phase (powerplay/middle/death)
- venue and season (scoring environments change)
- role (death bowler vs middle-overs controller)

## Common pitfalls

- Comparing economy across formats without context.
- Ignoring sample size (a few overs can be misleading).
