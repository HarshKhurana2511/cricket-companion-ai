# Bowling at the death (T20) — concepts

Death overs are typically the last 5 overs of a T20 innings. They are the hardest overs to bowl because batters are maximizing boundaries and fielders are spread.

## What “good death bowling” looks like

- **Limit boundaries** more than you chase dot balls.
- **Control lengths**: yorker length and hard-to-hit back-of-length are common plans.
- **Use variations**: slower balls, cutters, wide yorkers, and angle changes.
- **Predictability is punished**: repeating the same plan allows set batters to line up.

## Common ball types

- **Yorker**: targets base of stumps; harder to hit for six.
- **Wide yorker**: especially vs strong leg-side hitters; increases mishit chance.
- **Hard length / back of length**: used when yorker execution is risky; depends on pitch.
- **Slower ball**: disrupts timing; works best when disguised and used sparingly.

## Fielding concepts (high level)

- Fields are built around “where is the boundary risk?”
- Often you accept singles to protect boundaries.
- Changing fields can be used to influence batter shot selection.

## Analytics tie-in

Death-overs metrics should usually:
- exclude super overs (tiny sample, special rules)
- be based on legal balls for economy/rates
- distinguish bowler-attributed wickets from run outs
